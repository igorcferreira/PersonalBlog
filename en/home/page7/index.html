Body image

Feb 4, 2026

Warning: This text will talk about my view of my body. If you’re not interested in this, better skip this post. And if this is a sensitive subject for your mental health, better skip it too. P.s.: this article will contain references to nudity. For those who will stop here, here’s a GIF:



OK, if you’re still here, it’s because you intend to continue reading. So, here we go:

The last post I put on this site was about Asexuality. Now, I come to post something that is related to this topic, but not necessarily directly: body image. I’m using the term in English because I don’t know what the exact translation is for this concept. Besides, I don’t want the focus to be on the term, I’ll focus more on the feelings that surround this concept and how it impacted me, impacts me, and will still impact me a bit more.

I have a mental idea of “me” that doesn’t correspond to what I see in the mirror. I’ve felt this way for a long time, I’ve always accepted this idea and continued with my life. I’d like to point out here that it’s not the same challenge that a transgender person goes through, I accept that I am the same gender that my body presents. It’s just that the proportions and boundaries of my body have always seemed to me, in some way, wrong.

During adolescence and young adult life, I blamed the fact that I was too thin. Nowadays, I blame the fact that I’m a bit chubbier and out of shape. Never exactly “right” and having as the only common factor that I never knew exactly what that “right” would be. I never wanted to be very strong/defined, I think too much muscle is something kind of strange (regardless of gender); But at the same time, I don’t believe being thin/skinny is correct either.

I think it’s important to emphasize here that I have no problem with my body on a day-to-day basis, but when I see myself in photos/mirror I can’t exactly see that person as “me.” Something similar to when you hear your own voice in a recording for the first time: “You know it’s you, but that voice sounds strange. Everyone around you may be used to that voice, and still, to you, it sounds strange.”

I’m an introverted person, who doesn’t go to parties much, and travels alone. Therefore, photos of me (even more so when I started living alone) are rarities. And because they’re rarities, I kept “refusing” to take photos, to the point where I would get angry when someone took a photo of me, or asked to take a photo of me. Just like introversion and social phobia, I hide during events, but always uncomfortable and never wanting to see the result.

A fact about me: If I’m at home, alone, I’m always naked, because I find clothes somewhat uncomfortable and being naked is more comfortable. So, for a moment, I thought it was clothes that brought this questioning to my mind. But, after much thought, I don’t think so; since seeing myself in the mirror, even naked, still bothers me (although I don’t care at all about being naked). And this reinforces the idea that it’s not a discomfort with my body itself, because I don’t care how I actually look (how neat my beard is, or hair, or the clothes I’m wearing, etc). Because I never wanted to “please” anyone’s gaze, just wear what I like, the same way I believe all people are free to wear whatever they want (this part I believe is more related to asexuality).

During the pandemic, I started frequenting nudist beaches in Barcelona and near Barcelona more often. For two main reasons: There’s a nudist beach in Sitges that has waves (something I miss from the beaches of Brazil), and the beaches are generally emptier. I believe this is a privilege of being a man, since there are many perverts who go to this type of beach to “see naked women” (more noticeable/common among tourists here in Barcelona) and it’s safer for me to go to these beaches (but that’s a tangent for another day). And these trips to nudist beaches, again, raised this old question that I don’t care how my body looks to other people, I don’t care how I see other people’s bodies, but, for some reason, I care about how I see my body.

This questioning even kept me awake at night. And because of that, I decided to do a bit of “shock therapy,” taking multiple selfies of myself, with my phone and with a more “professional” camera. Both clothed and naked. To try to get used to the sight or, at least, understand what creates this disconnect between what I think of me and what I see/am.

Part of this shock therapy was creating a secondary account on Reddit and looking for non-“pornographic” nudity content/subreddits, to try to see if I could create an idea of what I understand as “ideal” (p.s.: finding this type of content of men is complicated, 99% of the content is penis photos). And, in the few subreddits I found, I tried to post some of my photos to get feedback, although in these communities there’s no one who will “criticize” you, so there’s no real feedback. Another part of this shock therapy is this post. Putting this thought and photos out there for “close” people virtually, so that my soul can relax a little knowing that something has been said.

That’s it. That was the afternoon thought.

Asexuality

Feb 4, 2026

This text will discuss something personal and will mention sexuality. If you’re not interested, I recommend skipping my next posts. That said:

Recently I’ve been posting about ADHD and mental health challenges. And something I’ve only recently resolved is asexuality.

Unfortunately, sexuality is a subject that isn’t debated as it should be (in my opinion). And I grew up in a sexist culture. Not in my home/family, but in my community. Brazil, to this day, is very sexist, and permeates the concept that a man needs to be “Macho.” And part of the fragile masculinity of the “macho” is that a man needs to constantly be desiring sex. Even when it extends to gay men, it’s still culturally expected that “men always think about sex.” And from there comes something that constantly affected my sleep/sanity; Despite identifying as a non-homosexual man, I could never identify as heterosexual. I won’t put myself on the same level of life difficulties as homosexuals or bisexuals, but it was always something that tormented my mind. And it brings me consequences to this day.

For most of my life, to fit into society, I lied to others (and to myself) about relationships. Seeking to say what others wanted to hear, while trying to convince myself that what I said must be the truth I sought. Especially since I’ve always had curiosities about sex. Curiosity of someone who is outside that world and would like to know more about it. Out of simple and pure curiosity. Something that made it difficult for me to understand my choices about relationships and sexuality. I’m very close to my 30s, and only in recent years have I accepted the concept of Asexuality, and accepted that I fit into this spectrum (yes, I believe sexuality is a spectrum, not a defined value. And much less binary). And I must say it was something very comforting.

Comforting because I could accept my curiosity about sex on the same level as my curiosity about cooking or music: What are the factors (social and biological) that lead a human being to prefer X over Y. This point still has an immense impact on my romantic relationships, because it involves expectations and desires. Something very important. But now, at least, it gives me full awareness of what should be debated, how it should be debated, why it should be debated, and with whom to debate.

For those wondering why this long text: Today, more than ever, I believe we (as a society) need to open up to debate sociological and mental health issues. And sexuality is part of that. And a very important part in the formation of a being’s identity. And sexuality, due to religion and conservatism, is something that is aggressively hidden and avoided. However, it plays an important role in human life, since it defines interpersonal relationships. And the understanding of the categorization of relationships.

And with that, I end my account. Kisses, and until next time.

First Third - Day 4

Feb 4, 2026

I stayed in Pamplona today, all day. And I believe it was a good idea.

I took advantage of the stop to wash a set of clothes, including my rain cape. It was my first time using a “self-service” laundry service. It’s something interesting. As a bonus, I had time to flip through a magazine about famous Spanish figures (nothing very different from the usual).

Another opportunity I had was to understand Spain’s mail system. I decided to look for stamps to send postcards I have with me, and discovered that there are 3 types of stamps: One for national shipping, one for shipping within the European Union, and one for international shipping, outside the EU. Each with its price and style. In the end, with a little help, I managed to buy the right ones.

I’m happy to be able to understand Castilian almost perfectly, even without being able to formulate a sentence currently. I’m using my Spanish as much as I can, in the attempt to, one day, be able to formulate a complete sentence without putting English or Portuguese in the middle. At the moment, I can already not starve! I know how to order coffee, orange juice, a sandwich, and water!

Today, the day honoring the Basque language was celebrated: Euskera. Until now, the entire route has been within the Basque Country, and, just like Catalonia, you can see the Basque flag in the streets and on buildings (alongside the Spanish flag, in some cases).

Editor’s note: So far, all the “cheese and ham” sandwiches I’ve ordered have been served with artisan cheese and Iberian ham! I have nothing to complain about.
