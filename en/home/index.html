A change

Feb 4, 2026

11 years ago I was moving from São Paulo to London. And 9 years ago I wrote the post So long… Thanks for all the fish!, where I talked about my move from London to Barcelona.

Now, a new change is happening in my life. At the end of March, I say goodbye to Future Workshops, a company where I’ve worked for the last 10 years. A decade that ends.

I say goodbye because the company has reached a point where I no longer identify with it. The projects, the clients, the challenges, and the day-to-day are no longer the same. The strategy of investing in the Saudi Arabia market, and the consequences of that investment, no longer make me comfortable being at the company.

At the beginning of this investment, I wrote the post Asexuality, and the desire to write that post came, in part, from the change in reality that is being involved in Saudi Arabian culture. For years I tried to understand the consequences and what was happening, and in the last 2 years I tried to motivate the company to end/change the relationship with the region. Now, it’s my moment to distance myself.

I still really like the people who work at the company, in London and Barcelona, and ending this relationship was not an easy decision, since I would like to still work with this team. But, in fact, it’s my moment to distance myself.

You, who read this here, and are not related to the company nor know the company, must be wondering: “And what does this have to do with me?”

My answer to you is: I don’t know.

I just wanted to write and publish my thought. Especially since, now, comes the biggest question: And what do I do now?

My job was a big factor for my staying in Barcelona. Living in Europe has great benefits. But I feel alone. I didn’t make friendships outside of work, and my family is an ocean away. It’s time to decide if staying in Europe is still an option. And even if I stay in Europe, whether staying in Barcelona is a good option.

I believe I’ll write again when I’ve decided, but I wanted to write that I need to think.

Writing from the past - Breakfast

Feb 4, 2026

29/10/2011 - Breakfast

He turned on the light out of habit for brightness. With dragging steps, he crossed the kitchen and opened the windows, letting the strong morning sun invade the space. He returned and let his hand fall on the switch, turning off the lamp without changing the brightness of the area. He stood still for a few more moments. His body and mind had not fully awakened and took every opportunity to stop and go back to sleep.

He gathered all the strength he had and prepared strong coffee. He went through the cupboards and fridge looking for accompaniments and fruits, and set up a complete and incredibly symmetrical feast for his breakfast.

He sat at the end of a long, wide rectangular table, with his back to the window so as not to blind himself with the light coming through it and to be comfortable. Besides, he enjoyed a nice breeze from behind and a pleasant counter-current of air caused by the nearby cupboards. He could still see all the other chairs. He was at the center and at the end at the same time. That was definitely the best spot.

Finally, he placed the cutlery carefully beside the empty plate and began to move and rearrange what was on the table to ensure symmetry. After a few minutes on the unnecessary task, he gave up and decided to start eating, even a bit unhappy with the color difference between the jelly and butter jars (which shared the center of the table). He served himself with the utmost care to maintain the position of things, relaxed, and let his mind fly with each piece of bread.

The memories, favored by the surroundings, took him to a Christmas party that had happened many years ago. This was still his parents’ house and he could indulge in the futilities of childhood. The rooms were full of relatives and friends eating and drinking and several children running all over. The pleasant traditional end-of-year chaos.

As always, he took care of realigning and redistributing cups, drinks, and sweets as they were consumed. The table (which now received his breakfast) kept the treats properly balanced and distributed, with the minimum of flaws possible. After all, he was still young, he couldn’t push himself too hard. If there were still this type of gathering at home, the organization would be perfect.

- A penny for your thoughts. - His déjà vu was interrupted by a sweet voice greeting him.

Standing at the door, a beautiful girl with brown hair and eyes stared at him. Her body was covered only by a short gray tank top and discreet panties, leaving her legs sensually on display.

- I just went back a few years in time.

- Hmm - The young woman mumbled, going to sit in the chair that was free right to the left, near the young man. - Nostalgia was never really your style.

- I see you don’t know me that well anymore. In the old days, you wouldn’t even need me to tell you what I was thinking.

- You pushed me away, made me distance myself from you. You believed the stories and advice from your family and stopped talking to me. It’s been many years.

- And I still can’t believe I’m seeing you again. I must be very desperate.

The young woman gave him an aggressive look, snorted, and stared at him for a few moments before gently placing her hand on the young man’s hand. He even thought about pulling back, but hesitated.

- A lot has happened. - the young woman tried to comfort.

There was much truth in those words. A lot had happened in recent days. The peaceful and sweet life ceased to exist and chaos took its place. Never had his mind been in such an intense whirlwind and things only seemed to get worse. There was no light at the end of that tunnel, only darkness.

Igor Castañeda Ferreira

Writing from the past - A crow once told me

Feb 4, 2026

15/09/2011 - A crow once told me:

Study, laws, question, learn to go after the solution to your problems. Try to find the peace of your spirit in the fullness of resolution. But don’t lose yourself in yourself. Have fun, have and make memorable moments. Throw yourself into lost hours with “nothing.”

Have friends. Even if few. But true ones. You’ll realize that you’re nothing, nor anyone, without them. Know that the most important thing in life is knowing that those who love you are and will always be by your side. A motivation for all suffering.

Family is the ultimate happiness. Not just the so-called “relatives,” but all who make you smile with a simple memory or just hearing their names. You are not alone in the world. And it’s these faces that will embrace your heart in a tender feeling called longing.

Be humble, strong, hardworking, and dedicated. Altruism is what sustains a real society. Seek your tranquility, even if it’s immersed in chaos. Know that you will find happiness in love. And, for that love, give yourself, even if it hurts. You won’t regret having “lived eternity” in someone else’s arms.

Upon fulfilling such objectives, be strong. They will take advantage of you. They will make you suffer. Your trust will be broken and your heart crumpled like paper. Bad times will come! And, for sure, they will pass.

Finally, one thing I assure you: “There is no formula for happiness.” Thank God. This is because it’s in every moment, every second. In the eyes of the one you love, in the hug of reunion, in the tears of goodbye… It’s up to you to see it.

Igor Castañeda Ferreira
